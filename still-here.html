Overview

Founded by Sonia Beyda Mosseri, designed in New York and sustainably produced in LA, Still Here is an up-and-coming fashion brand that combines durable fabrics with ultra-flattering shapes and, crucially, one-of-a-kind details. From cheerful embroidery to its signature hand-painted stripes, their designs are both head-turning and mood-enhancing.
‍
Still Here aims to stay true to the timelessness of wash and wear fabrics, yet by incorporating unique embellishments, the brand has managed to gain a large, organic following. With such aggressive growth, Still Here approached Class A Media to help create a digital presence that can speak to their followers as well as future customers.

Muted Minimalism

Working with Sonia to create the digital experience for their customers inspired a visual language that spoke true to the brand’s message, “novelty and consciousness”. Still Here prides themselves in working with high quality fabrics with small hints of delicate handiwork. We decided that the digital products their customers would experience shouldn’t be any different. Therefore, we worked with a minimalistic style of layout, typography and color. This way, the beautiful fabrics and embroideries would speak for themselves.
‍
Baskerville, the primary typeface is paired with Avenir Next to provide a sophisticated feel. While Baskerville - Bold Italic can be used in the newsletter pieces to provide a whimsical touch that can be taken seriously.
‍
The primary color, Alabaster, is a bone-white that helps lift the images and texts off the backgrounds while not being too visually intense on the eyes as opposed to pure white ( #FFFFFF). This, paired with the dark olive color for the CTAs creates a welcoming, mature touch that spoke directly to Still Here’s target audience: 25-45 year old working-class women.

The design of these mailing pieces resemble instant film sheets commonly used in old Fujifilm and Polaroid cameras that provide a vintage feel, thus, alluding to Still Here’s timeless values.

The Digital Experience

The website is where Still Here’s customers find themselves most often. It is imperative that we make sure the model’s images and clothing take center stage. The photography style helped immensely in this process. The chosen typography’s lightweight and small, consistent sizes as well as neutral, muted colors helped accomplish this feat. The negative space used throughout the site also allows the user interface to breathe.

Overall, the visual identity of the website is welcoming, relaxed and inviting to any potential customer who shops at Still Here.
